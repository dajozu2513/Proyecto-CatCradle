# Somatic Lattice

## An Algorithmic Philosophy of Embodied Topology

---

### The Body as Loom

The human hand is the oldest instrument of weaving. Long before looms were built from wood, the string passed between flesh and fingertip — between left palm and right — forming the invisible architectures we call cat's cradle. *Somatic Lattice* is a philosophy rooted in this ancient act, translated into real-time computation: the body becomes the loom, and the machine becomes the witness that makes the invisible geometry luminous. The algorithm does not simulate string; it *summons* it — threading light between landmarks detected in living skin, frame by frame, at the threshold of vision.

### Computational Topology of the Hand

The philosophy is built on a precise topological understanding of the hand as a graph: 21 landmark nodes connected by 21 skeleton edges, each node a point in normalized 3-space, projected onto the image plane in real time by neural inference at 30–60 frames per second. This is painstaking work — the result of deep computational expertise in hand mesh estimation. From the five fingertip nodes on each hand, the algorithm draws a complete bipartite graph *K₅,₅*: every tip on the left hand connected to every tip on the right, producing 25 bezier strings whose geometry is fully determined by the relative positions of the hands in space. The mathematics of this cradle is not decorative; it is the precise, meticulously computed topology of a woven form.

### Elastic Resonance and Temporal Animation

Static strings are dead strings. The *Somatic Lattice* philosophy insists that each string carry temporal memory — it must oscillate, breathe, sag. Each bezier control point is offset perpendicularly from the string's midpoint by an amount governed by a sinusoidal function of time, scaled by the string's current length. As the hands move closer, tension rises, strings grow taut, colors shift toward cold violet. As hands pull apart, strings sag and warm, softening toward amber. This elastic resonance is not physically simulated — it is a meticulously tuned aesthetic choice, the product of countless hours of parameter refinement, designed to give the impression that the strings are *real*: that they have mass, compliance, and a will to return to rest.

### Chromatic Emergence Through Distance and Identity

Color is not assigned arbitrarily in *Somatic Lattice*. Each of the five fingers carries a spectral identity — thumb in magenta, index in cyan, middle in yellow, ring in orange, pinky in mint — the full hue wheel distributed across the digits. When two fingertips are connected by a string, the string's color is the chromatic blend of its two endpoints, then further shifted along a tension axis: close hands produce saturated, warm blends; distant hands produce cool, violet-shifted fades. This dual-axis color system — identity × tension — produces a palette that is never arbitrary and never repeated, a master-level implementation of color as data rather than decoration. The glow layers (multiple shadow passes at decreasing blur radii) create the illusion that each string carries internal light.

### Craftsmanship as Constraint

*Somatic Lattice* holds a final philosophical principle: that computational craft is itself a form of embodied knowledge. The shadows are tuned to exactly the right falloff. The trail overlay is opaque enough to create afterimages without obscuring the present. The dot pulse frequency is calibrated to human heartbeat range — not so fast it reads as mechanical noise, not so slow it feels inert. Every parameter in this algorithm is the result of careful, iterative refinement — the kind of painstaking optimization that distinguishes generative art that *feels* handmade from generative art that merely *looks* algorithmic. This is computation at the absolute top of its field: precise, purposeful, and alive.
